üåü ConectaSalud (LLUM) - Remote Triage and PTSD Detection in Children (2025 - Ongoing)

Partnership: LabLENI (UPV) + Consorcio Hospital General Universitari | Agency: Consorcio Hospital General Universitari

Digital solution enabling effective remote triage for identifying children at risk of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) following traumatic events. The system was deployed in response to the 2024 DANA natural disaster in Valencia, collecting psychological questionnaires from children in Paiporta. Natural language processing analyses children's descriptions in their own words to evaluate psychological impact and facilitate early detection of mental health risks.

  • Real-World Deployment: Psychological assessment of children affected by the 2024 DANA flooding disaster in Valencia (Paiporta community), collecting trauma narratives and experiences in natural language
  • NLP for Trauma Assessment: Processing of children's free-text descriptions to extract psychological distress indicators, emotional language patterns, trauma markers, and PTSD risk factors
  • Remote Triage System: Digital platform enabling scalable psychological screening without requiring in-person visits, facilitating rapid assessment in post-disaster scenarios
  • Early Detection Focus: Identifying children at high risk of developing PTSD through linguistic analysis of their trauma descriptions, enabling timely psychiatric intervention
  • Clinical Integration: Direct communication channels with psychiatric units for critical cases, ensuring seamless referral and follow-up care
  • Technologies: Natural Language Processing (NLP), sentiment analysis, trauma-specific linguistic markers, psychological questionnaire digitisation, secure paediatric mental health data management
  • Scalability: Designed to be adaptable to other disaster scenarios and healthcare settings, promoting comprehensive children's mental health management beyond Valencia
  • Innovation: First NLP-based remote triage system for paediatric PTSD screening in natural disaster contexts, combining digital health with emergency psychological response, enabling large-scale mental health assessment whilst maintaining clinical accuracy
  • Current Status: Active project - ongoing data collection and analysis following the 2024 Valencia DANA disaster
